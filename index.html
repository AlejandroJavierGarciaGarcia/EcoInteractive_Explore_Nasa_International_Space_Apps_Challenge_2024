About

EcoInteractive experience

Welcome to EcoInteractive Hub, a platform dedicated to bringing you closer to the fascinating world of science and the environment in an interactive and entertaining way. Here you will find educational games that will allow you to explore the GLOBE Program protocols, learn about our planet and discover how your actions can make a difference.

EcoInteractive uses real-world data and scientific protocols to encourage the user to actively investigate their environment. The themes are designed so that users and especially children and young people have the integrity to get involved in this type of global initiatives.

  • Continuous learning for the benefit of the environment.
  • Innovation and commitment to promoting the GLOBE Protocol.
  • Improvement of the Earth's environment on a local, regional and global scale.

Our mission is to inspire and empower people of all ages to become agents of change, promoting environmental awareness and fostering a sustainable future for all.

​​​​​​Soils are one of Earth’s essential natural resources, yet they are often taken for granted.

Most people do not realize that soils are a living, breathing world supporting nearly all terrestrial life. Soils and the functions they play within an ecosystem vary greatly from one location to another as a result of many factors, including differences in climate, the animal and plant life living on them, the soil’s parent material, the position of the soil on the landscape, and the age of the soil.

Protocols

Water Temperature Protocol

Students will first measure water transparency at their undisturbed study site using a transparency tube or Secchi disk.

Carbon Cycle Commodi

Carbon Mission, an interactive game in which players become scientists who have discovered technology to miniaturize themselves and be able to study the carbon cycle in different ecosystems.

Soil Fertility protocol

Students will be able to play by preparing a mixture with chemical compounds to make their own fertilization and then with a kit to test the concentrations of nitrate, phosphate and potassium in the soil.

Water Vapor

In Steam Hunters, players are scientists from the future who have developed advanced technology to travel through time through sunlight waves and their goal is to collect boiling point data in the desert, jungle and Arctic.

pH Protocol

The planet Aquaterra is in danger from a mysterious force that has uncontrolled pH levels in aquatic ecosystems, threatening marine life. Players, as pH Guardians, must restore balance by measuring the pH of the water and using chemical solutions to neutralize the damage.

Clouds Protocol

Players manage a high-tech cloud factory, solving challenging puzzles and memorizing weather patterns from satellite images to simulate and strategically distribute various clouds in the Earth's atmosphere.

THE GLOBE PROGRAM

Frequently Asked Questions

GLOBE is a global science and education initiative that connects a network of students, teachers, and scientists to enhance the understanding and sustainability of Earth's environment. By engaging students in hands-on Earth system science, GLOBE fosters excitement for scientific discovery at local, regional, and global levels. To date, over 150 million measurements have been contributed to the GLOBE database, resulting in meaningful, standardized, research-quality datasets that support both student and professional scientific research.

Global Learning and Observations to Benefit the Environment.

GLOBE welcomes participation from individuals in partner countries. Teachers are equipped to guide students in data collection, and scientists collaborate with educators to enhance research. Organizations can assist in recruiting and training teachers.
GLOBE operates through agreements with the U.S. Government. For details on joining, contact the GLOBE Community Support Team at globehelp@ucar.edu.
